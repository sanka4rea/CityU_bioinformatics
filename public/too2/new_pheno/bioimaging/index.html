Super-resolution Imaging

In recent years, super-resolution optical imaging techniques have been developed to overcome the diffraction limit in fluorescence microscopy. Among these techniques, methods based on stochastic switching and localization of individual molecules, such as stochastic optical reconstruction microscopy (STORM) (Rust et al. 2006) and (fluorescent) photoactivated localization microscopy ((F)PALM), achieve sub-diffraction-limit resolutions by sequentially imaging and localizing fluorescent emitters in the sample. Cryo-EM

Cryo-electron microscopy of biological matter has made important advances in the past decades. It has become a research tool that further expands the scope of structural research into unique areas of cell and molecular biology, and it could augment the materials research portfolio in the study of soft and hybrid materials.
